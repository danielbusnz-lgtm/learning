Therefore, in general, here is the algorithm for computing the nullspace of any m × n matrix A (or
equivalently, for ﬁnding all solutions to the system of equations Av = 0):
• Write A in reduced row echelon form, and call the resulting matrix R
• Identify the pivot columns and the free columns of R; the pivot/free variables will be those xi
among x1, . . . , xn such that the i–th column of R is a pivot/free column
• The nullspace of A, which coincides with the nullspace of R, is spanned by those vectors
(x1, . . . , xn) where a given free variable is 1, all other free variables are 0, and all the pivot
variables are determined by the free variables and the equations:
