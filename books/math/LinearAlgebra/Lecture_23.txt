So what can we say about eigenvectors? First of all, eigenvectors corresponding to diﬀerent eigen-
values are always linearly independent. For example, if:
Av1 = λ1v1

the n eigenvalues of the matrix are all distinct numbers, because then there would exist n linearly
independent eigenvectors, one for each eigenvalue. These eigenvectors would form a basis of Rn.
