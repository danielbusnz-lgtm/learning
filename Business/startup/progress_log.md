### Key Concepts Covered

**Why Lean Startup Exists**
- Two failing extremes: "just do it" chaos vs rigid traditional management
- Traditional management fails for startups because of extreme uncertainty
- You don't know your market, customers, or product-market fit yet

**Where "Lean" Comes From**
- Toyota Production System / lean manufacturing
- Core principle: distinguishing value-creating activities from waste
- Biggest startup waste: building something nobody wants

**Car vs Rocket Metaphor**
- Rocket: plan meticulously upfront, tiny errors become catastrophic
- Car: steer constantly with immediate feedback
- Startups should drive like cars, not launch like rockets

**Build-Measure-Learn**
- The "steering wheel" for startups
- Constant adjustments based on customer feedback
- Unit of progress = validated learning, not shipping features

**Vision / Strategy / Product Pyramid**
- Vision: rarely changes (your "true north")
- Strategy: sometimes changes (called a pivot)
- Product: constantly changes (optimization/tuning)

**Other Concepts**
- "Achieved failure" = perfectly executing a flawed plan without testing assumptions
- Intrapreneurs = entrepreneurs inside established companies
- Author: Eric Ries (cofounder of IMVU)
